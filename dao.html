InterLink DAO — Governance by Verified Humans

Proposals, discussion, and voting — all anchored to human verification and on-chain transparency.

DAO Purpose & Principles

The InterLink DAO entrusts governance to real people. It ensures protocol updates, treasury allocations, and ecosystem policies reflect the will of verified participants rather than opaque institutions.

Core principles

  • Transparency — all proposals and votes recorded on-chain
  • Human governance — only verified humans participate
  • Accountability — governance outcomes are auditable and executed on-chain

How Governance Works

  1. Submit Proposal: any verified member can submit a proposal describing changes, budget, or actions.
  2. Discussion Period: community review, Q&A, and revisions.
  3. Voting: verified $ITLG holders cast votes during the voting window.
  4. Execution: successful proposals are executed on-chain or via DAO-managed multisig per the proposal’s spec.

Voting models (time-weighted, quadratic, or delegated) can be proposed and chosen via DAO governance to balance fairness and influence.

Voting Power & Sybil Resistance

$ITLG is the primary governance token. Because accounts are human-verified, InterLink prevents sybil attacks and ensures votes represent real individuals. The DAO can adopt additional mechanics — like stake-locking, reputation multipliers, or delegated voting — subject to community approval.

Treasury & Grants

The DAO manages a treasury for developer grants, partnerships, security audits, and ecosystem incentives. Grants are proposed, reviewed, and voted on transparently to accelerate real-world adoption and integrations.

Transparency & Auditability

All governance activity, proposal history, and on-chain executions are public. Anyone can verify outcomes and transaction traces to ensure accountability.
