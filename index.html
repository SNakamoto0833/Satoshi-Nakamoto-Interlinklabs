Enforcing Authentic Agency: Top-of-Block Transaction Priority

In the evolving landscape of Web3, the integrity of a decentralized network hinges on its ability to distinguish genuine human activity from automated or malicious bot interference. The InterLink Chain addresses this fundamental challenge head-on with its groundbreaking Top-of-Block Transaction Priority mechanism, a direct manifestation of our Proof of Personhood (PoP) philosophy. This ensures that authentic human agency is not merely a theoretical ideal but a core, enforced principle of the network‚Äôs operation.

The Challenge of Bots in Decentralized Systems

Traditional blockchains often treat all transactions equally, processing them based on gas fees or arrival time. While seemingly fair, this neutrality inadvertently opens the door to Sybil attacks, bot-driven market manipulation, and the distortion of decentralized governance. Automated actors can flood networks, inflate activity metrics, front-run human trades, or monopolize voting power, eroding trust and undermining the very principles of decentralization.

How Top-of-Block Priority Works

The InterLink Chain‚Äôs solution is embedded directly into its block construction algorithm. When a transaction is submitted, the network first verifies the sender's identity through their InterLink ID.

  • Verified Human Transactions: Transactions originating from an InterLink ID-verified Human Node are identified as high-priority. These transactions are systematically elevated and placed at the "top" of upcoming blocks by the network's validators.
  • Unverified/Automated Transactions: Transactions from addresses not linked to a verified InterLink ID, or those identified as automated (e.g., bot activity), are processed after human-verified transactions. They are relegated to lower priority slots within the block or, during periods of high network congestion, may experience delayed inclusion.

This dynamic prioritization ensures that real human activity, whether it's a critical governance vote, a social interaction in a Mini-App, or a time-sensitive financial transaction, consistently receives expedited processing.
